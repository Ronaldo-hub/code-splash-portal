About the Khoisan First Nations

The Khoisan are the indigenous people of Southern Africa with a history dating back approximately 140,000 years. Their cultural heritage, languages, and traditional knowledge represent some of humanity's oldest living traditions.

Did you know? Some Khoisan languages have fewer than 100 fluent speakers remaining. The mandate calls for urgent preservation efforts to protect this linguistic heritage.

The Khoisan Voice Mandate

The Khoisan Voice initiative advocates for indigenous rights through four key pillars:

Land Sovereignty

Recognition of ancestral territories and complete resource rights based on historical occupation dating back 140,000 years.

Cultural Recognition

Preservation of languages and cultural heritage, including stopping the use of colonial terms like 'coloured'.

Political Representation

Direct representation in governance and veto power on legislation affecting Khoisan territories and rights.

Financial Reparation

Addressing historical injustices through community development funds and economic compensation mechanisms.
